“I have the upmost confidence because I’m religious about avoiding false confidence.“I’ve been told to have more confidence a few times in the past. Each time this has been a revelation that the person didn’t understand my mentality. I can’t describe my reaction to this idea or how it makes me feel. I guess I just think “oh, you’re very mistaken.” As far as I know, I’m the most confident person that I know. What is confidence if it isn’t founded in truth? This is why I’m the most confident person that I know. I don’t know anyone who has more ambitious goals and has prepared themselves accordingly. I don’t know anyone who is less concerned with external opinion or perspective with regards to the accomplishment of a desired task. To be clear, you frameSurely, my circle is limited and no meaningful conclusion about by confidence level can be drawn except that it is relatively high.  Why do I get to say that I’m the most confident person that I know? For starters, because I don’t actually know anyone else’s confidence level actually. But moreso, it’s a sefish, biased viewpoint. I’m confident because of my accomplishments.I’m confident because I didn’t become subject to my environment. That has installed the blelief in me that I am above any environment. Lucky, I logically understand that there have been many a man to overcome much greater obstacles. But there obstacles aren’t mine, and so I the lack of familiarity to it makes mine more powerful to me.I’m also confident because of my perspective, not just my belief. Many people require power and resources to be confident. Others can be confident with only understanding. Some people are fine with losing the first three quarters. I know something that you don’t about the fourth.Lately, I’ve been thinking about why I was such a confident basketball player.How could I be such a confident player with such low shot attempts on average? Well, I always understood that I wasn’t a scorer. I never looked at myself as such. That wasn’t my philosophy of the game, or the way I desired to play it. Though, I later to realize that it’s the most fun role and can be a beneficial one. I didn’t understand this then. I just wanted to play the best to win the game. I wasn’t backing down to anyone. We would battle until the buzzer.I think I owe this to those before me. I had been subject to a few basketball cultures where this was the norm. The grit was expected. You had to play with pride. An unwillingness to allow your opponent to score.
