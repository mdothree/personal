Lessons From Gigalyze

Latarence Butts
May 22, 2021

My second year of college in college I took 18 hours both semesters so that I could finish the next year. I wanted to create something that would have an immediate impact on my surroundings. I noticed how many people asked around for assignments at school. I also noticed that different schools have their own drive. 
I saw an opportunity to allow students to be compensated and better educate themselves. Every engineer knows the power of a practice test or an old exam. Plus, most classes of high difficulty will give you a practice exam. That’s because even with the practice exam, you still need to solidly the concepts. If a number changes or a form of the question changes, you have to be able to solve a question using the necessary principles. In a good course, remembering won’t help. You need understanding.  
Gigalyze gave me confidence. If I hadn’t change from Fead to Gigalyze, I have continued down a long path that had no fruitful end in the short term. After Gigalyze, I had a clean landing page and a working platform. I just hadn’t pulled the plug on marketing. That’s because I didn’t want to feel responsible for any negative outcome. I worked on it through Summer 2017. After Summer, we threw a launch party. We ran a few ads here and there. But we hadn’t even began to understand advertising at the time.
More clearly, my heart wasn’t in it. I went deep enough to the point that I wasn’t dedicated. That’s an important point. I wasn’t dissuaded by the lack of desire to continue. I had known that these were vehicles, and not my destination. Still, I expected them to succeed. 
As I reflect, I’m embarrassed and angered by my naivety and unpreparedness in that time. I had tried to do everything on my own. Any advice wouldn’t been foreign language. I think about this often. What’s the separator between us? Is it time? Experience?  Mindset?
